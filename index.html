About

I'm the Co-Founder and CTO of Morph Labs, where I build the AI that decodes non-invasive brain signals into real-time motor control. The field has spent decades trying to preprocess EEG into something it's not. We took a different approach. Train on the noise, not against it. I'm working towards brain foundation models: universal representations of neural dynamics. Many modalities, all of the understanding, and none of the surgeries. That's the long game.

I taught myself to code in fall 2023, dove into deep learning, and then into computational neuroscience. By the time I graduated in May 2025, I had gone from writing my first lines of Python to building real-time neural decoding systems. That self-taught path shaped how I think about the field. The most impactful neurotech should be accessible to everyone, not locked behind operating rooms and research labs.

I believe deeply in education and in learning out loud. The best way to push a field forward is to bring others along with you, and I try to do that through my writing, my work, and every conversation I have about the future of brain-computer interfaces.
